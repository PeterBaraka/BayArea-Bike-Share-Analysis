What is the structure of your dataset?¶

The dataset has the following structure: There are 183412 rows of bike usage data with 16 features. There are both numerical and non-numeric values with the minority being nun-numeric i.e. start and end station names, user type, gender and bike share for all status.

The following derived features were added to aid in the depth of the data exploration: duration_minute, start_date, start_hour, start_day, start_month and member_age

What is/are the main feature(s) of interest in your dataset?¶

I intend to find out what the most common trip is per user type as well as the various metrics relating to it from the day, month and hour. I also intend to find the most common trip start and end destination to infer the possible central locations that act as hubs linking all other stations.

What features in the dataset do you think will help support your investigation into your feature(s) of interest?¶

The trip location (start and end stations), duration (month, day, hour e.t.c) and the particular user type will help inform the results of this investigtion. While the user based data will help inform the kind of demographic that utilizes the service.

Conclusions¶

The primary findings from this exploration were: Subscribers utilize the service the more than customers with their peak usage being on weekdays in the morning hours (around 6:00 am to 8:00 am) and afternoon hours (4:00 pm to 6:00 pm) with significant drops over the weekends. We may infer that most subscribers use the service to get to their work destinations and then get back home at the end of the day. We also see that subscriber trip duration is concentrated around 20 minutes and below versus customers who have longer trips (although they are fewer).
